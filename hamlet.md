# A beautiful tale of love through grief

A very heartbreaking story, told with so much care and restraint that a large portion of the audience was in open tears, grieving what many of us already knew was coming.

I especially loved the story told through Agnes’s and Will’s clothing. The shift from her bright red dress - so full of freedom and life - to softer, muted tones as she settles into the homestead, and finally to the complete loss of color after Hamnet’s death, felt like a perfect visual expression of the cycle of her energy and spirit. In contrast, Will’s wardrobe grows heavier and more layered, with leathery textures that feel like a shell he builds around his grief.

Then, seeing their clothing return to their original states - her red, flowing dress and his light, free white gown as the ghost - felt like a quiet but powerful sign of their reconciliation with Hamnet’s death. It’s such a beautiful, emotional way to show healing without ever having to say it out loud.
